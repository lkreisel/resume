Linking Superfund Hazardous Waste Sites with Medicare Treatment Data

This project links the location of Superfund hazardous waste sites in the United States with hospital-level Medicare treatment data to determine whether residents of counties containing Superfund sites were more likely to seek medical treatment for certain conditions.

Identifying Emerging Stock Market Themes

This project uses Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning to identify outperforming emerging themes in the stock market. Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) topic modeling was performed on 10-K forms pulled from the SEC API. The goal of this project was to find themes that transcend industry labels.

Detecting Homes Flipped without a Permit in Washington, DC

This project identifies which homes were flipped in Washington, DC without any renovation permits being pulled. The question of whether the number and type of permits pulled can add value to the final sale price is also explored.

Read More
Visualizing 311 Requests in Washington, DC

This project contains a series of visualizations of 311 requests in Washington, DC at the Census tract level. 311 requests are juxtaposed against Census tract median household income. The question of whether lower income Census tracts have longer ticket response times is explored.
